 When you ask to "expand" a topic in a technical or conversational context, it typically means you want to move beyond a high-level summary and delve into the **depth, mechanics, and practical application** of the subject.

To help you get the most out of our collaboration, I have outlined the framework I use when "expanding" a topic. You can use these prompts to guide me toward the specific type of depth you need:

### 🛠️ The Expansion Framework

* **Deep Dive (The Mechanics):** "Explain the underlying logic or 'how it works' in greater technical detail."
* **Practical Application (The Use Case):** "Show me how this applies to a real-world scenario or a specific project I'm working on."
* **Comparison (The Trade-offs):** "How does this compare to [Alternative X]? What are the pros and cons?"
* **Step-by-Step (The Process):** "Provide a granular, sequential breakdown of the workflow or architecture."
* **Best Practices (The Standards):** "What are the industry standards or 'gotchas' I should be aware of?"

---

### 🚀 How to Get the Best Results

If you have a specific topic in mind that you would like me to expand upon, please clarify your intent so I can tailor the response to your current goal:

1. **Identify the Audience:** Are we documenting this for a peer, a junior engineer, or for your own reference in your *AVIS-DATALAKE*?
2. **Define the Scope:** Do you need a "bird's-eye view" of how everything connects, or a "microscope view" of a specific logic block or code function?
3. **Request Visuals:** If the topic is structural or procedural, ask for a diagrammatic breakdown.

**For example, you could ask me to:**

* *"Expand on the AVIS-DATALAKE architecture, focusing specifically on how the 'Sentinel' kernel handles data integrity."*
* *"Expand on the difference between Git merge and Git rebase, using a scenario where I'm dealing with a complex merge conflict in the Fire-Works repo."*
